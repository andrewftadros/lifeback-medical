## How Can You Prevent Cancer?

Most types of cancer can be detected with early screenings. For your convenience, we perform all testing at our center in Corona.

### Types of Cancer Screenings

- Blood work
- Skin checks
- Upper endoscopy
- Tissue biopsy
- Urine testing

## The Link Between Obesity and Cancer

Several recent studies have established a link between being overweight and developing cancer. Many people mistakenly believe that cancer is predominantly an inherited condition. While every individual has an innate or acquired susceptibility to specific types of cancer, in the majority of cases this susceptibility leads to actual cancer development only after being driven by one or more external risk factors.

One such risk factor is obesity. Steadily on the rise in America, obesity has recently overtaken tobacco use as the number one preventable cause of cancer deaths in the United States.

## Colonoscopies

Colon cancer is the fourth most commonly diagnosed type of cancer. A colonoscopy uses a 4-foot long, flexible tube about the thickness of a finger, equipped with a camera and light at its tip. The procedure begins once the patient is fully sedated.

The camera tube is inserted and provides a live feed of the rectum, colon, and cecum (the top portion of the colon). This allows doctors to identify precancerous tissues, polyps, and any abnormalities. Colonoscopies can also diagnose the cause of symptoms such as blood in the stool, abdominal pains, or diarrhea, and may be used as a follow-up procedure to clarify findings from a colonic X-ray or CT scan.

If you are over 50 years old, have had any type of cancer in the past, or if a blood relative has, you should take advantage of this important diagnostic and preventive tool.

**In its early stages, colon cancer is one of the most curable cancers. In its later stages, it is the second most deadly.**

Regular screening saves lives. Speak with our team to schedule your colonoscopy and take a proactive step toward cancer prevention.
