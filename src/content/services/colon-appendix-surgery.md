## Why Would You Need Colon Surgery?

The colon is a large area and can be prone to a number of diseases. You could need a resection surgery because of colon cancer, IBS, appendicitis, diverticular disease, gastrointestinal bleeding, a large bowel obstruction, or intestinal polyps.

## Why Would You Need Your Appendix Removed?

Our appendix helps us maintain healthy intestinal bacteria, serving as the body's original probiotic. However, it can become infected. This infection is called appendicitis, and once infected, the appendix can burst, releasing bacteria and stool into the abdomen and causing an even worse infection. Appendix removal is the quickest way back to health.

## Colon & Appendix Procedures

### Colon Resection

There are two types of colon resection procedures: a traditional open surgery and a minimally invasive laparoscopic surgery. The type of procedure performed depends on each individual's diagnosis and needs.

During a colon resection, the diseased part of the bowel is removed and the two healthy sections of the colon are reattached. This is called an end-to-end anastomosis.

If an anastomosis is not possible because of the extent of the disease or its location, your surgeon creates a colostomy. A temporary colostomy is made when the colon needs longer to heal after surgery, and the anastomosis is then performed later.

### Appendectomy

A laparoscopic appendectomy is the minimally invasive surgical removal of an infected appendix. Depending on the severity of the infection and whether or not the appendix has ruptured, a patient might need a more traditional open surgery.

The appendix itself is a small pouch attached to the beginning of your large intestine on one end and sealed shut at the other. While it is a small organ, if someone gets appendicitis, they must have their appendix removed immediately. The infection can spread rapidly, and if it causes a perforation, it can infect the entire abdominal area. This condition is called peritonitis and is a life-threatening emergency.

## Minimally Invasive Expertise

Our surgeons specialize in laparoscopic techniques for both colon and appendix procedures whenever possible. Minimally invasive surgery offers patients smaller incisions, less pain, reduced risk of infection, and significantly faster recovery times compared to traditional open surgery. Our team will evaluate your specific condition and recommend the approach that gives you the best path to recovery.
