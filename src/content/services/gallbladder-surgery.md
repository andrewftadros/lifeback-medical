## You Can Live Without It

Gallbladder removal is extremely common, as the human body does not need it to survive. Recovery time is usually one week and you go home the same day of surgery.

Your gallbladder is a small organ that lives under your liver. It stores extra bile your liver makes and releases it after you eat to help your body digest and absorb the vitamins from fatty foods. However, normal digestion is totally possible without the help of the gallbladder.

## Gallbladder Disease & Gallstone Symptoms

If you are experiencing any of the following symptoms, your gallbladder may be the source of the problem:

- Abdominal pain
- Nausea
- Indigestion
- Fever
- Vomiting
- Jaundice

## Gallbladder Removal Surgery

If you are having symptoms of gallbladder disease or have been diagnosed with gallstones, the only way to safely treat the condition is to surgically remove the gallbladder. The disease does not go away on its own and will not respond to medication. Our doctors can help you determine if your gallbladder is the source of your issues and get you back to feeling your best.

### Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy

The most minimally invasive gallbladder surgery is called a Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy. Our doctors remove your problem gallbladder using only four small incisions in the abdomen. The laparoscopic approach requires less recovery time and causes less pain than traditional open surgery. Our doctors can determine if you are a good candidate for this less invasive treatment.

## What to Expect After Surgery

Typically, patients will feel some pain and nausea right after surgery but will be able to leave the hospital once they can tolerate liquids. You are encouraged to walk as soon after the surgery as you feel comfortable doing so, as this helps you heal.

Most patients are able to get back to daily life about one week after laparoscopic surgery, whereas patients who have open surgery will need four to six weeks for recovery. After surgery, you will follow up with your surgeon in two weeks and then get to go on with normal, pain-free living.
