## What Is GERD?

Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) affects over 160 million people. It occurs when stomach acid and food flow backward up into the esophagus instead of moving down into the stomach.

## GERD Symptoms

Common symptoms of GERD include:

- Frequent heartburn
- Nausea and vomiting
- Chronic hoarseness and sore throat
- Regurgitation
- Sleep apnea
- Acidic and bitter taste in the mouth
- Tooth decay
- Non-cardiac chest pains
- Bad breath
- Burping
- Difficulty swallowing

## GERD Treatments

### The Anti-Reflux Procedure

A minimally invasive laparoscopic fundoplication, also known as the Anti-Reflux Procedure, is covered by many health insurance plans because GERD is a very common condition. The procedure requires only a few small incisions, which is what makes it minimally invasive.

A laparoscope, which is a thin, lighted tube with a camera on the end, is inserted through small incisions in the abdomen or pelvis. It provides a live view of the surgery so that your surgeon does not need to perform an open procedure. This means you will heal more quickly and get heartburn relief sooner.

The fundoplication surgery works by strengthening and building up the barrier between your stomach and esophagus. By reinforcing this barrier, it becomes nearly impossible for stomach acids and food particles to flow back up into the esophagus, effectively addressing the symptoms of GERD.

## Why Seek Treatment

Recent studies conducted on more than 200,000 U.S. Veterans suggest that people who use common heartburn drugs for months to years may face heightened risks of death from heart disease, stomach cancer, and kidney failure. Surgical treatment offers a lasting solution that can reduce or eliminate the need for long-term medication use.
