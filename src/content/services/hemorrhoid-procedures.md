## Hemorrhoid Diagnosis & Procedures

Pain and bleeding during bowel movements can last a lifetime. The effects of at-home remedies, over-the-counter medicines, and high-fiber diets are only temporary or may not work at all. With our treatments, we provide permanent relief.

## Diagnosis Comes First

Our doctors need to diagnose your hemorrhoids before treatment can begin. We use one of three minimally invasive methods to get you answers quickly and pain-free.

Using either a colonoscopy, a sigmoidoscopy, or a proctoscopy, our specialists will identify your issue and determine the most effective treatment. Once we know whether you are suffering from hemorrhoids, an anal fistula, an anal fissure, a perirectal abscess, or pilonidal cysts, we can move forward with the right approach.

## Hemorrhoid Treatments

### Band Ligation

This procedure eliminates internal hemorrhoids one to two at a time over the course of a couple of weeks. It also scars the tissue where the procedure was done, making it nearly impossible for hemorrhoids to return to that area.

Rubber band ligation involves your doctor using a viewing instrument called an anoscope to visualize the internal hemorrhoid. Another tool is then used to grab the hemorrhoid and tie it off at its base with a tight, tiny rubber band. Once this is done, blood can no longer flow to the hemorrhoid, causing it to shrink and eventually fall off.

Our specialists will eliminate all your hemorrhoids in four-to-six week intervals. They can treat more than a couple at once if you undergo general anesthesia. The procedure is performed in the office, and you can either rest for a couple of days or go right back to your daily life, minus the heavy lifting.

### Hemorrhoidectomy

The removal of hemorrhoids via laser or scalpel typically occurs in the following situations:

- Very large internal hemorrhoids
- Internal hemorrhoids that cause symptoms after non-surgical treatment
- Cases where band ligation has failed
- Large external hemorrhoids that cause significant pain and discomfort

Our doctors perform a hemorrhoidectomy by making small incisions around the anus. You will be placed under local or general anesthesia depending on your needs as discussed with our doctors. You can usually go home the same day, and recovery takes a couple of weeks.

The long-term success of your hemorrhoidectomy depends heavily on following up with changes to your daily fiber intake and bathroom habits. Regardless of the surgery, making it easier for your body to have regular bowel movements is essential for lasting results and overall digestive health.
