## Do Your Knees or Back Hurt?

Simply going up a staircase increases the pressure on your knees by four times your body weight, so it is no wonder that knees are the most commonly injured joint in the body.

Working a typical 9-to-5 job without stretching every 30 minutes can cause significant strain on your back. Other everyday factors like sleeping on an old mattress or carrying extra weight, whether inside your body or on top of it from an overloaded backpack or purse, can lead to chronic back pain.

## Pain Elimination Treatments

### Weight Loss

Carrying extra weight puts stress on all the body's joints and challenges our basic anatomy. If you are overweight and experiencing significant pain in your knees and back, our doctors may suggest a medically supervised form of weight loss as your treatment.

This will not always mean surgery. It can simply mean working with a dietician and being guided by a doctor who specializes in weight loss.

### Pain Injections

For both knees and backs, injectable anesthetics provide effective and immediate relief for chronic pain sufferers. These procedures can be performed at all of our office locations or surgery centers.

The process typically involves a local anesthetic to numb the injection site, followed by a contrast dye viewed through X-ray to pinpoint exactly where the issue and inflammation are located. There are three main groups of pain injections, all of which are minimally invasive and most are covered under health insurance plans.

### Knee Arthroscopy

Arthroscopy is the best minimally invasive method to both diagnose and treat tissue and tendon damage in and around the knees. While patients are placed under general anesthesia, one to five small incisions are made and a tiny camera called an arthroscope is inserted into the knee area. This allows our surgeons to view the inside of the joint and identify and repair problems.

The majority of knee arthroscopy procedures take 30 minutes to an hour to perform. Recovery from any type of knee surgery usually involves crutches, physical therapy, and extended recovery periods.

If you are living with chronic knee or back pain, schedule a consultation to explore which treatment option is right for you.
