## Do You Experience Sleep Disorder Symptoms?

Common symptoms include restless sleep, depression, headaches upon waking, irritability, memory loss, loud chronic snoring, daytime sleepiness, dry mouth or sore throat in the morning, gasping for air and choking during sleep, frequent nighttime urination, and pauses in breathing during sleep.

Over 42 million American adults have sleep breathing disorders, and 1 in 5 have mild obstructive sleep apnea. An estimated 80% of people with sleep apnea do not know they have this dangerous disease.

## What Is Sleep Apnea?

Sleep apnea is a sleep disorder in which the sufferer experiences abnormal pauses in breathing during sleep. These pauses can happen 30 or more times in a single night and last anywhere from a second or two to nearly a few minutes.

The most common type, obstructive sleep apnea, is approximately 80% more likely to affect people who are overweight and about 50% more likely in those with diabetes compared to individuals at a healthy body weight without diabetes.

Because sufferers essentially stop breathing without being aware of it, sleep apnea can have serious side effects or even deadly consequences. If a family member has told you that you stop breathing during sleep, or if you experience many of the symptoms listed above, you should participate in a sleep study.

## Types of Sleep Disorder Studies

### General Sleep Study

This overnight diagnostic study can be conducted at home or at a sleep center. You will work with one of our sleep technologists who conducts a polysomnogram. Electrodes placed on your head, chest, and legs record brain activity, eye movements, heart rate, blood pressure, oxygen levels, body movement, and more. Our doctors use this data to diagnose sleep disorders and provide treatment options.

### MSLT Study (Nap Study)

The MSLT study is conducted the same way as a general diagnostic study but usually follows an initial sleep study where further testing is indicated. It is used to help diagnose narcolepsy and to accurately identify individuals who feel tired all day, every day. Pinpointing these sleep-related symptoms improves the quality of both rest and wakefulness.

### CPAP/BiPAP Titration Study

This overnight study is conducted with patients who have already been diagnosed with sleep apnea and are using a CPAP or BiPAP machine. The study determines the best settings for your specific device. If you have any difficulties with pre-existing equipment, we can improve its function or get you set up with updated equipment.

### MWT Sleep Study

The Maintenance of Wakefulness Test measures your ability to stay awake and alert during daily work functions. Our doctors are commonly retained by transportation departments, commercial trucking companies, and shipping companies to conduct MWT studies on drivers and operators who need clearance to drive. These tests ensure the safety of those who operate heavy machines and vehicles, and everyone who shares the road with them.

## Types of Sleep Disorder Treatments

### CPAP Machine

CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) therapy involves a small motorized box with a hose and face mask. The machine draws in air, filters and humidifies it, then delivers it through the mask to your nasal passages. A proper mask fit is critically important for comfort. If you already have a CPAP machine but find it too uncomfortable to use, let us know and we can explore whether a different mask will work better for you.

### Weight Loss

Being overweight contributes to the severity of many sleeping disorders. Medically supported weight loss can help take your sleep and life goals to the next level. This does not always mean surgical measures are needed. Whether it is surgical weight loss, creating a meal plan with a dietician, or regular physician follow-ups, we partner with you to support your overall health.

### Uvulectomy

In some patients, extra tissue in the uvula may be blocking the airways. Removal through a uvulectomy can resolve certain cases of chronic snoring and sleep apnea. This surgery is not usually a first option, and non-surgical methods are always prioritized.

Because sleep is critically important to overall health and impacts your ability to work, many health insurance plans cover sleep study testing.
