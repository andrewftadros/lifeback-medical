## Spider Veins, Varicose Veins & Reticular Veins

More than 20% of the population suffer from spider, reticular, and/or varicose veins. There are valves in all our veins that play an important role in circulation. Arteries carry blood away from the heart to get it oxygenated, and veins return this oxygen-enriched blood back to the heart, completing the cycle of circulation.

When vein valves malfunction, they allow blood to flow backward into the vein instead of pushing it upward toward the heart. This backflow causes blood to pool in the vein, stretching it and producing the enlarged, blue-colored veins we recognize as spider, reticular, or varicose veins.

## Minimally Invasive Vein Elimination Treatments

### Radiofrequency Ablation

The latest in minimally invasive vein ablation technology eliminates painful and problematic reticular and varicose veins using radiofrequency energy. These veins are deemed medically necessary to treat, so this option is best suited for more than solely cosmetic vein issues.

The Venefit procedure, in which a vein specialist inserts a ClosureFast endovenous radiofrequency ablation (RFA) catheter, has been shown to be 93% effective. The procedure is performed in-office with a local anesthetic, eliminating the need for groin surgery and general anesthesia. There is little to no scarring, and it has been proven to restore vein circulation up to four times faster compared to laser-based catheters.

Our vein specialists insert the ClosureFast catheter into the vein through a tiny incision below the knee. Using ultrasound imaging, the physician treats the faulty vein segment with a 20-second burst of radiofrequency energy, causing the vein to shrink around the catheter. The catheter is then drawn out, continuing treatment on each segment until the entire length has been sealed. An average 45 cm vein can be treated in just 3 to 5 minutes. Once the vein is sealed shut, blood naturally reroutes to healthy veins.

Patients are encouraged to walk right after treatment and can return to their routine, avoiding strenuous leg activity for up to two weeks. This procedure offers less bruising, instant relief, and fast recovery.

### Laser Ablation

Endovenous Laser Ablation (EVLA) eliminates spider and varicose veins by sending strong bursts of light onto the vein until it disappears. Clinical studies show that EVLA has a 98% success rate on the first treatment with exceptional long-term results.

The procedure is performed in-office with only a local anesthetic. A duplex ultrasound maps out the vein and allows our doctors to target the faulty valve. A thin laser fiber is then inserted through a tiny entry point, usually near the knee. Light from the laser seals the vein shut and redirects blood flow into a healthy vein.

Patients can walk immediately after the procedure and return to everyday life, with a brief wait before more rigorous activities like running or going to the gym. Some soreness and bruising may occur but can be managed with over-the-counter pain medication.

### Sclerotherapy

Doctors can also eliminate painful or cosmetic spider, reticular, and varicose veins through sclerotherapy and foam-based sclerotherapy. A liquid or foam-based solution is injected directly into the vein, causing it to scar from within and collapse. Blood is then forced through healthier veins, improving circulation as the faulty vein tissues are reabsorbed by the body.

This procedure is performed in-office without any anesthetic and takes about an hour. It is more commonly used for cosmetic cases. While it is an effective standard in many vein treatments, results may take longer to appear and some cases may require multiple injections.

Patients can walk immediately after the procedure and return to everyday life, though gym activity should be postponed briefly. Compression stockings are worn for a couple of weeks to support healing.

### Stripping & Ligation

Vein stripping and ligation involve surgical removal of the damaged vein through more traditional but still minimally invasive techniques. These procedures do not require hospitalization and use regional or general anesthesia.

Stripping or ligation is typically used for large varicose veins, to prevent venous skin ulcers from returning, or when legs ache, swell, and feel heavy after standing. It may also be recommended when varicose veins have begun to bleed, when ulcers develop, or when the damaged vein is located where it joins superficial veins in the knee or groin.

The procedure involves making an incision below the damage, inserting a flexible instrument up the vein, and removing the damaged portion through another incision above the vein. The cut portion is then tied off (ligated) near the incisions. In some cases, ligation alone redirects blood flow and eliminates the need for stripping.

Patients undergoing stripping or ligation experience a recovery period of 5 to 8 weeks and should stay home for a few days following surgery, though most go home the same day.
