## Surgical & Non-Surgical Weight Loss Options

Life Back Medical offers a full range of weight loss treatments, including both surgical and non-surgical options. Our surgical procedures include LAP-BAND, sleeve gastrectomy, gastric bypass, revision surgeries, and conversions. Non-surgical options include gastric balloon placement and dietician-managed meal planning with physician oversight.

Your work PPO plan can help cover the costs of your procedure. For Kaiser and HMO patients, we offer discounted rates.

### Am I a Good Candidate for Weight Loss Surgery?

When you come in for a consultation, our doctors will discuss the different types of procedures, your goals, and together you will decide on the best course of action for your particular situation. Our doctors take into account your current health, any underlying conditions such as diabetes, sleep apnea, or heartburn, and your desired goal weight.

## Weight Loss Treatments

### Sleeve Gastrectomy

Sleeve gastrectomy is the surgical middle ground in weight loss operations. It requires no implantation, resulting in less risk of slippage, erosion, and food intolerances compared to a LAP-BAND. It also carries lower health risks than a full gastric bypass while delivering similar results.

Using minimally invasive surgical techniques, patients recover about a week faster than with a gastric bypass, with a turnaround of approximately two weeks. The procedure removes a portion of the stomach, leaving behind a small sleeve roughly the size of a banana. Part of the stomach that produces the hunger hormone is removed, which means patients may not feel hungry as often. A smaller stomach means eating less, and eating less means weighing less.

### Gastric Bypass

Gastric bypass is one of the most common forms of weight loss surgery in the United States because it produces reliable weight loss with acceptable risks and minimal side effects. Our surgeons staple off a large section of the stomach, leaving a tiny pouch that can only hold a few ounces of food. This results in patients feeling hungry less often and eating much smaller portions.

Ideal gastric bypass candidates have 100 pounds or more to lose and have tried other weight loss methods without success. A lifelong commitment to a diet and supplement regimen after surgery is necessary, including adequate amounts of lean protein, B12, iron, and calcium. Patients should avoid sweets and fatty foods after the procedure.

### Revision Surgeries

Bariatric surgery is one of the most effective long-term weight loss solutions for those who have struggled with excess weight and obesity. Sometimes the expected results are not achieved and complications can develop. Our board-certified surgeons are experts at detecting the problem and getting you back on track toward your ideal weight.
